2.On the practical side of things, you may want to consider setting up an Health Savings Account (HSA) or Flex Spending Account (FSA) account to make goal #1 feasible.

If you've had a high deductible plan insurance plan this year or a health insurance plan that does not cover many out-of-network services, you may want to consider setting up a HSA or FSA account for next year.

Funds deposited in your HSA or FSA account can be used for balances that exceed your medical benefits, or for office visits to various practitioners such as your dentist and yes, you guessed it ... even your chiropractor!

What is a HSA account?

A Health Savings Account is a medical savings account available to US taxpayers who have a high deductible health plan. The funds contributed to the fund are pre-tax dollars, meaning they are not subject to federal income tax and can be used for various health services and products. Funds that are not spent at the end of the tax year will automatically roll over to the next.

What is a FSA account?
A Flex Spending Account is medical savings account similar to an HSA account, but differs mainly in two ways: 1) Funds must be spent by year end and DO NOT roll over; and 2) It is usually set up by an employer/company in contrast to an HSA which is individually owned.

Your HSA account can be easily set up through your health insurance broker and likewise, your FSA can be set up through your employer.
