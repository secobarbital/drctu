Health Tip:

Keep your joints moving

How much time do you spend sitting?
--Consider your commute to and from work, errands, or to pick up the kids?

How much time do you spend sitting, staring into your computer screen?
--(Time to fess up accountants, engineers, and lawyers!)

How much time do you spend sitting to share a meal or catch a TV program?

If standing is considered 100% of the mechanical load

to your lumbar discs, did you know that a forward

slumped 30 degree position increases the load to 360%??!!!

Tally up the number of hours you sit all day ---Now that's a lot of stress to your spine!

You're probably asking..."So what's your point, Dr. C?"

My point is...Wear and tear doesn't necessarily come from running a 26 mile marathon! There is accumulative daily wear and tear on your joints just from sitting, driving, slouching, lifting, and bending. Now, not unless you're a park ranger, your typical desk jobs is stagnant enough for your body's composition to change overtime, and for the worst. (Both soft tissue and bony changes occur!)

Accumulation and severe stress to the body's joints can lead to a condition called Osteoarthritis. --the deterioration of joints. Typically, this is what is happening when you see an older individual crouched over--Their body eventually fuses into this position and is NOT necessarily a result of age! Rather, it's a result of not maintaining proper joint health, mobility, and postural alignment.

To read more about Osteoarthritis, click here
(Though the article was geared for seniors, Osteoarthritis can start at any age depending on load, history of trauma, predisposing conditions such as scoliosis, poor posture, etc.)

Here are a few tips on how to keep your joints moving and how to preserve them over the years...

1.Stretch daily (For stretches that would suit your body type and/or condition, please ask!)

2.Exercise regularly and vary your regime. Having variation in your workout regime allows for various muscles to be strengthened and thus protecting your joints more readily.

3.Keep moving! If you're at a desk job, make a point to stand throughout the day, pace around your office, use a ball or a sitting disc!

4.Learn core stabilizing exercises such as abdominal bracing and four-point stance. (ASK!!)

5.Start better postural habits Did you know that neutral positioning of your spine segments minimizes the internal stresses and degree of muscular effort required to maintain normal alignment? That is why Chiropractic helps with not only alignment, but greater ease in having improved posture.

6.Stick with your maintenance health plan, whether it's regular chiropractic care, massage therapy, acupuncture, etc.


Chiropractic helps to keep joints moving and promotes alignment.

This helps to decrease improper load and stress to joints.
