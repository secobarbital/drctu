The key to great restorative sleep is to keep the spine neutral in whichever lying position you are in. There is no magic pillow nor magic bed - choose something that meets your body's needs and body type. I can help you with this.


If you own a feather pillow, throw it out! Feather pillows offer the least amount of support.


Avoid sleeping on your stomach. Tummy sleepers tend to crank their necks during sleep. This can lead to many neck problems and symptoms such as waking with numb or tingling arms and fingers.


When sleeping on your back, try supporting your legs with a large pillow. This allows your lower back to rest gently against the surface of the bed.


When sleeping on your side, place a pillow in between your legs. This helps to keep the pelvis "square" and prevents twisting of your low back.
