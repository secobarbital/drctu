Whether you’re just feeling achy and sore, or if you’ve taken a fall or tumble, it’s usually best to start with ice.  Pain is typically a side-effect of the process of inflammation.  More often than not, initially heating an injured area can set back the healing process.  Ice helps to minimalize inflammation.  Remember, our bodies are all slightly different...Ask if you are unsure whether to ice or not. 

Here are a few general tips to ice properly.

  1. 1.Use a good gel pack or a bag of crushed ice. 

  2. 2.Make sure your skin is protected from the ice/gel pack.  Usual a paper towel or thin t-shirt placed between you and the ice works well.

  3. 3. Ice for 15-20 minutes (numb) depending on the area of the body.  Sensations of cold, burning, and stinging are common before  the feeling of numbing. 

  4. 4. When icing, be in a neutral, resting position.
