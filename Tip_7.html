1. Instead of three traditional meals (breakfast, lunch and dinner), break up your meals into 5 smaller meals throughout the day. By feeding the “brain” more consistently both in quantity and frequency allows your body to process energy more efficiently. This also helps to prevent the “sluggish” feeling a.k.a “food coma” that typically follows a big meal.


2. Cut back on refined sugar.                                                     You’ll find these in cookies, candy, cereals etc. Try to keep your carbohydrate intake to the early potion of your day. Again this is easier for your body to process. Fruits and vegetables contain “natural” sugars that are great energy sources for the brain.

  

3. Increase fruits and vegetables in your diet. They are a good source of fibre. With any well-balance diet, we need fibre to move food in and out of our bodies efficiently. Ask me about the CORN TEST.

Please remember that each of us is built differently. With that said, we process foods and respond to exercise with variation. Do what is in balance for your body.
