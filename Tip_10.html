What is "wellness care"??

Perhaps we should start the conversation with “What is the opposite of wellness care?”  A simple example is what I call “Band-aid” treatment: come into the office and fix what hurts or treatment based on your signs and symptoms.


“Band-Aid” type of treatment  or Intensive care (phase I) often does not allow me to truly get to the root of the problem or make significant correction as in Reconstructive care (phase II).


I often compare Wellness care (phase III) to preventative, elective dental procedures (teeth cleaning), and practices such as exercise, healthy eating, meditation etc. Wellness care is maintenance designed to ensure your skeletal structure is maintaining itself as it was taught to in reconstructive care.


X-ray films provide a blueprint of your spine and show what kind of damage or degeneration has occurred to your spine. Having a base-line x-ray film of a region of your spine allows me to to tailor a wellness program specific to you, so to prevent any further breakdown.



I have seen the paradigm shift in many of my patients and their families over the last few years of practice. I congratulate those on electing wellness care and I encourage those who are thinking about it, to do so.
