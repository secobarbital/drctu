I typically take a quick glance as you waltz or hobble into my office for posture and gait. Often, I will even inspect the soles of your shoes for uneven wear, compare your leg length from right to left, and also check for hip rotation. (You, yourself may even notice one foot flaring or pointing out more so than the other.)

All these things are often caused by bad postural habits and faulty biomechanics. To make the equation worse, they are also usually compounded by poor foundation. What do I mean by "poor foundation?" --I'm referring to the fact that your body may lack support from your shoes and can cause your shoes to wear unevenly, resulting in foot, knee, hip or chronic and/or general body discomfort. Though there are different solutions ranging from a simple "Dr. Scholl's" to "Super Feet," a customized orthotic is often an excellent solution to having tailored correction and support.

FAQs

Can orthotics help me even if I don't have discomfort or pain? __ Peak performance requires optimal biomechanics and excellent foundation. Along with good supporting shoes, orthotics can minimize additional stress, fatigue, inefficiencies, and loss of energy. As you age, your body changes. Orthotics are a good way to support healthy arches in the feet and to continue to promote healthy gait.

Who makes orthotics? __ Although I have cast orthotics for my patients in the past, I now refer the majority of patients to Adam Kemist, a Certified Pedorthist who uses the same AmFit computerized system that I used to use.

How long will they last? Depending on the platform material chosen, orthotics can last for several years. I typically recommend a platform made of EVA (Ethylene Vinyl Acetate--the same material that is used in the mid-sole construction of most sport shoes) opposed to a hard plastic/fiberglass platform. I prefer a material that moves with your body rather than work against it.
